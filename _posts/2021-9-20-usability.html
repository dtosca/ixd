Good interface: the Beauty Blender

What it's for

A sponge for putting on makeup.

I sent a picture of a Beauty Blender to my friend to test his makeup skills. I asked him "What is this?" and he said "A pink teardrop."

Good things:

  • Affordances: being able to hold it and squish it.
  • Because it's made of a spongey material, you can assume it's abosrbant.
  • Pointed tip as a signifier for getting makeup into crevices.
  • Wider base for applying makeup to larger areas.
  • It's light pink color allows the user to know when it's dirty and needs to be cleaned.

However...

  • It's not obvious that you're meant to wet it before using it so that it doesn't absorb as much makeup product.

Bad interface: a stove designed by Satan

What it's for

Heating things up to cook them and causing house fires.

I once left the stovetop burner running all night because I couldn't tell it was turned on.

Bad things:

  • Learnability and discoverability is terrible! Figuring out how to turn on the oven was a nightmare.
  • Feedback for when the oven is turned on is obscured from the user.
  • No signifiers for when the oven has reached the desired temperature.
  • If the oven fire turns off in the middle of baking, there is nothing to show that it is cooling down (which has ruined my brownie batches before)!
  • Mapping of the dials to the stovetop is ass.
  • Nothing to signify if the stovetop is still hot.

I'll give it one thing:

  • The stovetop dials don't just turn, you have to push them in to turn them, which prevents accidentally turning the burners on.
